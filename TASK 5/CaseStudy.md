## CYBERCRIME CASE STUDY

Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers trick individuals into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or OTPs. This is usually done through fake emails, messages, or websites that appear legitimate.

A typical phishing attack begins when a user receives an email that looks like it is from a trusted source, such as a bank. The email may contain a warning message, asking the user to click on a link to verify their account. Once the user clicks the link, they are redirected to a fake website that looks identical to the real one. When the user enters their login details, the attacker captures the information.

Students and young adults are common targets because they are active online and may not always verify sources. The consequences of phishing include financial loss, identity theft, and unauthorized access to accounts.

This type of attack is dangerous because it relies on human error rather than technical flaws. Awareness and caution are the best ways to prevent phishing.
